David Gilmour

the voice and guitar of Pink Floyd

David Gilmour and Roger 'Syd' Barrett met as children in Cambridge, UK, and later began playing guitar together. In 1965 Syd joined Roger Waters, Richard Wright, and Nick Mason to form Pink Floyd, while David continued playing with a succession of his own bands. In 1968 David was asked to augment the Pink Floyd line up as the singer and guitarist, only for Syd to leave the group five gigs later. David's guitar playing, singing and song writing became major factors of Pink Floyd's worldwide success, including his distinctive vocals and guitar playing on The Dark Side Of The Moon, the third most successful album of all time.

David Gilmour Facts

  • David Jon Gilmour, the first child of Douglas and Sylvia Gilmour, was born on March 6, 1946, in Cambridge, England
  • He borrowed his first guitar from a neighbor when he was 14 and never returned it. In an interview from 2008 he said that he still has that guitar
  • He has a houseboat called the Astoria, it was built a century ago. He bought it in 1986 and then turned it into a recording studio. Most of his work over the last two decades was mixed and assembled aboard the Astoria.
  • David lists "Wish You Were Here" as his favourite Pink Floyd album and "Shine on You Crazy Diamond" as his favourite Pink Floyd song
